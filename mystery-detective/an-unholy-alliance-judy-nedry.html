Like many women in their fifties, Emma Golden feels invisible. She lives quietly in her Portland, Oregon bungalow and minds her own business. But her tranquil life is about to change. She is asked to return to the rolling hills of her former wine country home south of the city to supervise a friends bed and breakfast inn near Dundee.

Emma arrives at the Westerly Inn during grape harvest. She is under contract to write a book about Oregon wineries, and its business as usual until she discovers one of her subjects dead in a wine vatmurdered at his own dinner party.

Cougar Crossing Winery owner Ted Maxell was a ruthless and dishonest newcomer to the northern Willamette Valley wine scene. Many people wanted him goneincluding his son, many local winegrowers, and even Emmas ex-husband, Dwight. Then Maxells daughter, Tiffany, calls Emma and begs for assistance. I know who killed my father, she wails. When Emma answers Tiffanys cry for help, she finds herself drawn into the search for a murderer or murderers with secrets worth killing for.
