  • Beyond the High Blue Air is a gorgeously written and vital literary contribution to the conversation surrounding assisted deaths, the right to die, the ambiguity of loss, and the tenuous nature of consciousness.
  • The honesty and urgency in Spinney’s narrative comes through clearly; the author pursued an MFA for the specific goal of writing this memoir. Spinney seamlessly moves between the voice of a personal memoir, a sharp neuroscientific narrative, and Miles’s imagined life.
  • Many readers will take comfort and shared solace in Spinney’s story; Beyond the High Blue Air is a raw, powerful account of grief, loss, and maternal love that is at once specific to Spinney’s circumstances and universally relatable.
  • The memoir has already gotten rave reviews, excerpts, and broad media attention in the UK; the same acclaim is expected of its US release.
  • Spinney is a strong and articulate advocate for death with dignity as a result of her family’s tragedies and is comfortable speaking/writing on the topic; she already has ties to Death with Dignity organizations in the UK and can leverage her knowledge and connections in the US.
