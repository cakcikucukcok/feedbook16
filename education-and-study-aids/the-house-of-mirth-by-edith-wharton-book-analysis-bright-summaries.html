This engaging summary presents an analysis of The House of Mirth by Edith Wharton, which tells the story of the beautiful, witty Lily Bart, a young woman from a wealthy family who has fallen on hard times. Although she is seemingly determined to restore her fortunes by attracting a wealthy husband, Lily's romantic side gets the better of her when she falls in love with Lawrence Selden, while the machinations of those around her threaten to destroy her reputation for good. The House of Mirth is one of the best-known novels by the American writer Edith Wharton, who became the first woman ever to win the Pulitzer Prize in 1921. She is considered one of the most influential female writers of the early 20th century.
